Pasta Bolognese

Description

Pasta Bolognese is a classic Italian dish featuring a rich, slow-simmered meat sauce (known as ragù alla Bolognese) made with ground beef, pork, and sometimes veal, combined with a soffritto of onions, carrots, and celery, tomatoes, wine, and milk or cream

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Onion, garlic, carrot
  • Red wine
  • Canned or peeled tomatos
  • Dried herbs

Steps

  1. Prepare the soffritto: Sauté finely chopped onions, carrots, and celery in olive oil until soft and translucent.
  2. Cook the meat: Add ground beef (and pork/veal if using), pancetta, and garlic; brown thoroughly, breaking up the meat.
  3. Deglaze with wine: Pour in red or white wine and simmer until mostly evaporated.
  4. Build the sauce: Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes or passata, broth, and herbs (like oregano or thyme).
  5. Simmer slowly: Cover partially and simmer for 1 to 4 hours to develop deep flavor—longer cooking yields richer results.
  6. Cook the pasta: Boil fresh or dried pasta (like tagliatelle or penne) until al dente.
  7. Combine and serve: Toss the hot pasta with the sauce, adding reserved pasta water to adjust consistency. Serve immediately with freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano.
